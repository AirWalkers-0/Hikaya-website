# Design System: HIKAYA

## 1. Overview

**Creative North Star: "Moroccan Intelligence, Cinematic Storytelling"**

HIKAYA's visual system should feel like a premium AI photography atelier rooted in Moroccan atmosphere. It combines royal-blue calligraphic identity, cloud-white space, mist-gray softness, sand-beige warmth, zellige detail, horseshoe arches, viewfinder marks, and editorial photography composition.

The site is not a tech product and not a generic portfolio. It is a cinematic sequence: first a full-bleed Moroccan hero, then a tactile three-step process collage, then an editorial works gallery, then a calm project inquiry moment. The design should make visitors feel that ordinary client source imagery can become a refined campaign world.

The system is elegant without being empty, Moroccan without becoming touristic, and AI-powered without looking technological. It should feel crafted, precise, luminous, and culturally specific.

**Key Characteristics:**

- Royal blue as the signature brand color.
- Cloud-white and mist-gray foundations with sand-beige warmth.
- Blue HIKAYA English/Arabic logo used consistently.
- Moroccan motifs integrated as frames, dividers, tiles, seals, lens marks, and borders.
- Cinematic full-bleed imagery paired with quiet interface controls.
- Collage sections that explain process through visual sequencing.
- Minimal editorial portfolio layouts adapted to the HIKAYA blue-and-Moroccan system.
- Scroll pacing that feels authored and remains readable without animation.

## 2. Colors

The palette is luminous, Moroccan, and precise. Royal blue carries identity and interface emphasis; neutrals create air and softness; sand and terracotta add warmth without taking over.

### Primary

- **HIKAYA Royal Blue** (`#001BFF`): The core identity color. Use for the logo, primary buttons, active navigation, section labels, sparkle motifs, viewfinder corners, fine dividers, icon line art, and selected project numbers.

### Supporting Blues

- **Deep Ink Blue** (`#061A3A`): High-contrast text on light surfaces when black would feel too harsh.
- **Soft Lapis Mist** (`#7D8CFF`): Secondary blue for subtle gradients, dotted AI lens patterns, hover traces, and delicate ornamental marks. Use sparingly.

### Neutrals

- **Cloud White** (`#F6F7FA`): Primary page base. It should feel light, photographic, and airy.
- **Mist Gray** (`#C7CCD6`): Secondary surface, quiet dividers, disabled states, and soft UI lines.
- **Warm Off-White** (`#FBF6EF`): Alternate surface for portfolio and process sections when warmer contrast is needed.
- **Ink Charcoal** (`#10131A`): Main readable text color.
- **Muted Slate** (`#5E6472`): Body copy, captions, metadata, and inactive navigation.

### Warm Accents

- **Sand Beige** (`#E7D6C1`): Moroccan plaster, paper, collage notes, and subtle background waves.
- **Light Terracotta** (`#C48A7A`): Occasional accent for handmade warmth, seals, annotations, or secondary markers. Never replace royal blue as the main brand color.

### Named Rules

**The Royal Blue Ownership Rule.** HIKAYA is identified by royal blue. The logo, important UI moments, and editorial markers should remain blue across the site.

**The Cloud Not Cream Rule.** The base can be warm, but it should stay luminous and airy. Avoid beige quiet luxury that flattens the brand.

**The Moroccan Warmth Rule.** Sand and terracotta should appear through architecture, paper, product texture, and handcrafted details rather than broad flat color blocks.

**The No Tech Glow Rule.** Do not use neon gradients, blue-purple AI effects, robot iconography, synthetic dashboards, or circuit visuals.

## 3. Typography

HIKAYA uses a mix of elegant display typography, refined sans-serif navigation, and Arabic calligraphic presence through the logo.

### Recommended Pairing

- **Logo / Wordmark:** Custom HIKAYA English and Arabic lockup in royal blue. Use the original calligraphic logo in the cinematic sections. A simplified all-caps wordmark may be used only in minimal portfolio contexts when the full logo would be too ornate.
- **Display Font:** High-contrast editorial serif for large headlines and section titles. References: Cinzel Decorative, Canela, Freight Display, Editorial New.
- **Subheading / Label Font:** Refined uppercase serif or semi-bold sans with generous tracking. References: Cinzel SemiBold, Optima-like forms, or a precise grotesque.
- **Body Font:** Clean humanist/geometric sans. References: Montserrat, Söhne, GT America, Inter with careful tracking.
- **Handwritten Accent:** Optional for process collage notes only. Should feel like art-direction annotation, not casual doodling.

### Hierarchy

- **Hero Display:** Large, centered, royal blue serif. Used for "Intelligence" and major emotional statements.
- **Section Title:** Large serif or refined display type in royal blue or deep ink blue.
- **Project Title:** Small uppercase or title-case sans/serif, restrained and readable.
- **Metadata:** Uppercase, tracked labels for project category, step numbers, and navigation.
- **Body:** Short, calm, readable, and never overexplained.
- **Arabic:** Present in the logo lockup and optional ornamental labels. Arabic typography should feel intentional and dignified, not decorative filler.

### Named Rules

**The Blue Serif Authority Rule.** Major claims can be blue and theatrical, but supporting text must stay simple and readable.

**The Logo Consistency Rule.** In the original hero/process language, use the blue HIKAYA English/Arabic logo rather than a plain black wordmark.

**The One Voice Per Line Rule.** Do not crowd display serif, sans labels, and Arabic calligraphy on the same line. Let one voice lead.

## 4. Layout System

### Global Rhythm

Use generous spacing, cinematic alignment, and editorial asymmetry. The site should feel composed rather than gridded mechanically.

### Preferred Section Directions

#### Hero

- 16:9 full-bleed cinematic Moroccan terrace image.
- Top-left blue HIKAYA English/Arabic logo.
- Top navigation across the upper center: Home, Portfolio/Work, Services, About, Contact.
- Top-right royal-blue **Book a Session** button.
- Center headline: "Where Art Meets Intelligence, Stories Become Legacy."
- No central CTA buttons.
- Lower-left circular/arrow **Scroll Down** CTA.
- Use viewfinder corner marks and sparkle motif in royal blue.

#### Process / What We Do

- Collage board direction.
- Three-step narrative:
  1. Client Flat Lay
  2. Artistic & Creative Direction
  3. Final Product
- Use taped paper, product photography, zellige swatches, handwritten art-direction notes, moodboard sketches, and final campaign image.
- Arrows should guide the eye from raw client input to creative transformation to final polished output.
- Blue numbers and labels should anchor each step.

#### Our Works

- Use a 16:9 editorial layout with staggered vertical project images inspired by the selected minimal reference.
- Preserve original HIKAYA identity elements: blue logo, blue accents, Arabic logo presence, small stars, Moroccan motifs, and fine ornamental lines.
- Avoid a generic all-black minimal top bar when the page belongs to the original HIKAYA system.
- Project cards can vary in height and position; avoid uniform square grids.
- Include project category metadata: Product Photography, Hospitality, Editorial, Campaign, Architecture, Interior, Lifestyle.

#### Contact

- Calm, earned, and premium.
- May use a Moroccan arch frame, blue CTA, warm paper surface, or cloud-white panel.
- Keep form fields quiet and readable.
- Copy should focus on starting a story, campaign, or visual world.

## 5. Motifs & Brand Elements

### Core Motifs

- **Viewfinder Corners:** Thin royal-blue corner marks around logos, hero headline, image frames, or CTAs.
- **Sparkle Motif:** Four-point star used as separator, active nav indicator, and small emphasis mark.
- **AI Lens Mark:** Circular aperture/lens form used as icon or subtle background graphic.
- **Zellige Pattern:** Blue, beige, and white tile pattern used for borders, collage scraps, section dividers, and small swatches.
- **Eight-Point Star:** Moroccan geometric symbol for icons, process marks, and ornamental separators.
- **Horseshoe Arch:** Used as image mask, frame, hero architecture, or contact container.
- **Mashrabiya Lattice:** Subtle tonal texture, never overpowering.
- **Cloud / Mist Texture:** Soft atmospheric background layer for continuity with the reference style.

### Motif Rules

Motifs must serve structure. Use them to frame, separate, guide, or brand the interface. Do not scatter them randomly.

One section should not use every motif at once. Choose two or three per section and let the photography lead.

## 6. Elevation & Surfaces

HIKAYA should feel tactile but not SaaS-like.

- Use paper collage, pinned photos, tape, and wax-seal-like marks in process sections.
- Use flat editorial surfaces for works sections.
- Use very soft shadows only where a paper/photo object needs separation.
- Avoid glassmorphism, heavy blur panels, floating SaaS cards, and decorative shadow boxes.

### Named Rules

**The Photographed Paper Rule.** Collage elements should feel physically placed, lit, and photographed.

**The Editorial Flatness Rule.** Portfolio layouts should feel printed and composed, not like card components dropped into a template.

## 7. Components

### Navigation

- Desktop: top-left logo, centered nav, top-right action.
- Logo: blue HIKAYA English/Arabic lockup for brand-rich sections. Simplified wordmark only for very minimal portfolio experiments.
- Active nav: blue star, small underline, or royal-blue label.
- CTA: royal-blue filled rectangle with crisp radius and white text.
- Mobile: concise, thumb-friendly, fast-loading, with logo and Book/Contact action visible early.

### Buttons

- **Primary:** Royal blue fill, white text, crisp rectangle, small radius.
- **Secondary:** Blue outline or text link with arrow.
- **Scroll CTA:** Circular icon plus text, lower-left or lower-center depending on section.
- **Hover:** Subtle movement or blue intensification. Disable movement under reduced motion.

### Cards / Project Items

- Image-first.
- Minimal text: number, title, category.
- Optional arrow on hover.
- Avoid identical square-card grids.
- Use staggered sizing and white space to create authorship.

### Process Cards

- Torn paper or soft handmade paper surface.
- Blue step number.
- Short title and 2-3 lines of copy.
- Optional tape, pin, zellige swatch, handwritten note.
- Must clearly guide the user through the three-step flow.

### Icons

Use line-art icons derived from the brand motifs:

- AI lens
- Eight-point star
- Zellige tile
- Viewfinder corners
- Sparkle
- Arch
- Aperture

Icons should be royal blue or mist-gray, never multicolor tech icons.

## 9. Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- **Do** keep HIKAYA royal blue central to the identity.
- **Do** use the original blue logo system in brand-rich website sections.
- **Do** integrate Moroccan motifs as frames, borders, patterns, and visual logic.
- **Do** make the hero cinematic and atmospheric.
- **Do** make process feel tactile, guided, and easy to understand.
- **Do** make works feel editorial, curated, and premium.
- **Do** use generous whitespace without losing the brand's blue presence.
- **Do** optimize images for mobile and support reduced motion.
- **Do** plan English/French support and keep RTL possible for future Arabic content.

### Don't

- **Don't** remove the original logo personality from sections that need brand continuity.
- **Don't** make the top bar look like an unrelated black minimal fashion template when it should match the HIKAYA hero and process sections.
- **Don't** use generic AI visuals, robots, neon gradients, or dashboard language.
- **Don't** let beige minimalism erase the royal-blue identity.
- **Don't** overuse Moroccan patterns until the design feels ornamental or touristy.
- **Don't** use uniform square portfolio grids.
- **Don't** make the process more than three steps unless the product strategy changes.
- **Don't** rely on tiny text, low contrast, or motion to communicate key content.

## 10. Accessibility & Implementation Notes

- Target WCAG AA contrast for all text and controls.
- Royal blue on cloud white is generally strong, but small uppercase labels must be tested.
- Text over photography needs overlays, placement discipline, or adequate contrast.
- Support `prefers-reduced-motion` for parallax, scroll reveals, hover motion, and collage animations.
- Use responsive image formats and lazy loading for all heavy photographic sections.
- Build bilingual strings from the start for English and French.
- Keep CSS layout direction flexible enough for possible Arabic/RTL expansion.
- Avoid hardcoded left/right positioning where logical properties would work better.
