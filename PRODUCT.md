# Product

## Register

brand

## Product Definition

HIKAYA is an AI photography agency that creates elevated visual stories for Moroccan and MENA brands. The site is a hybrid between a cinematic landing page, an editorial portfolio, and a light inquiry experience. It is not a software product, a dashboard, or a technical AI tool.

The website should make HIKAYA feel like a premium creative studio that uses AI quietly in service of art direction, cultural atmosphere, and campaign-quality imagery. The visitor should never feel they are buying generated images. They should feel they are commissioning a visual world.

## Users

HIKAYA serves three primary visitors.

The first is a founder, brand owner, or marketer at a beauty, fashion, hospitality, lifestyle, or product brand. They may already have basic product photos or flat-lay imagery but need those assets transformed into a campaign world that feels premium, emotional, and commercially useful.

The second is a creative director, marketing lead, or social media manager at an established local brand. They already understand production value and are evaluating whether HIKAYA can replace, supplement, or accelerate traditional shoot workflows without losing polish. They need confidence signals: taste, consistency, cultural fluency, process clarity, and final-output quality.

The third is a hospitality, interior, or luxury service business that wants imagery with Moroccan atmosphere: riads, arches, zellige, warm stone, courtyard light, refined product scenes, and editorial storytelling. They are looking for visual identity, not a generic content vendor.

All visitors are asking the same question: can HIKAYA make my brand look more iconic than it currently does?

## Product Purpose

The site exists to make HIKAYA feel like the obvious call for brands that want high-end AI-assisted photography with Moroccan soul. It should prove that the studio can turn simple client input into refined visual storytelling.

Success is an emotional sequence:

First, cinematic trust. The hero should create immediate belief through atmosphere: Moroccan architecture, deep royal blue, soft cloud light, and the HIKAYA logo system. The visitor should feel that the studio already has a point of view.

Second, creative clarity. The process section should make the offer easy to understand without making it feel mechanical. The chosen process is three steps:

1. **Client Flat Lay / Input Imagery** — the client sends product shots, flat lays, references, or source visuals.
2. **Artistic & Creative Direction** — HIKAYA develops the visual world through styling, composition, Moroccan references, storytelling, AI enhancement, and art direction.
3. **Final Product / Delivery** — HIKAYA delivers polished campaign-ready imagery for websites, social media, brand storytelling, ads, and launch assets.

Third, portfolio conviction. The works section should showcase best projects in a clean editorial layout inspired by the selected minimal reference, while keeping HIKAYA's original royal-blue identity, Arabic logo presence, Moroccan motifs, and star/viewfinder details.

Fourth, confident inquiry. By the time a visitor reaches contact, they should not be asking whether HIKAYA can create premium imagery. They should be deciding what product, campaign, or brand world to start with.

The governing feeling is: **where art meets intelligence, stories become legacy.**

## Brand Personality

Elegant. Cinematic. Moroccan. Crafted. Intelligent.

HIKAYA should feel like a visual atelier rather than a tech company. It should be warm, refined, and culturally rooted. The brand should project taste before explanation, atmosphere before mechanics, and proof before persuasion.

The tone should be calm and assured. HIKAYA does not need to over-explain AI. The AI is a hidden engine; the visible output is beauty, storytelling, and campaign value.

## Design Language Summary

The design language established in the brand kit is royal blue on cloud-white and mist-gray foundations, supported by sand beige, light terracotta, Moroccan zellige, arabesque geometry, horseshoe arches, mashrabiya lattice, camera/viewfinder corner marks, sparkle motifs, and AI lens marks.

The website should preserve the identity created in the first sections:

- Blue HIKAYA logo with English and Arabic wordmark.
- Cinematic Moroccan terrace hero in 16:9 format.
- Minimal top navigation with Home, Portfolio/Work, Services, About, Contact, and a blue Book a Session action.
- Blue editorial typography and delicate star/corner motifs.
- Moroccan craft details used as system elements, not decoration.
- A "Scroll Down" CTA in place of heavy hero buttons.

## Core Sections

### 1. Hero

A cinematic full-bleed Moroccan terrace view with a woman in royal blue facing the city, framed by arches and zellige. The logo stays blue. The main copy remains:

**Where Art Meets Intelligence, Stories Become Legacy.**

The hero should avoid two central CTA buttons. Instead, the top-right navigation keeps **Book a Session**, while the lower-left action becomes **Scroll Down**.

### 2. What We Do / Process

The preferred direction is a collage-style process board. It should feel tactile and editorial, using taped paper, product flat lays, zellige fragments, handwritten notes, moodboard references, and final campaign imagery.

The process must be three steps:

1. **Client Flat Lay** — client sends raw product imagery and source visuals.
2. **Artistic & Creative Direction** — HIKAYA transforms the input through styling, composition, storytelling, Moroccan atmosphere, AI enhancement, and visual strategy.
3. **Final Product** — polished campaign-ready assets are delivered.

This section should guide the user visually from raw input to refined output.

### 3. Our Works

The preferred works direction is the minimal horizontal editorial gallery inspired by the last selected reference, but adapted back into the original HIKAYA visual system. It should use a generous 16:9 layout with staggered vertical project cards, refined whitespace, royal-blue details, Arabic/English logo presence, and Moroccan motifs.

Project examples may include:

- Hikaya Skincare / Product Photography
- Riad Al Nour / Hospitality Visuals
- Atlas Beauty / Editorial Portraits
- Sahara Skin / Product Story
- Nomad House / Architecture
- Amane Studio / Interiors
- Timeless Morocco / Heritage Campaign

### 4. Contact

Contact should feel earned and calm, arriving after enough proof. It may use an arched frame, blue button, warm surface, and short copy focused on starting a project rather than filling a generic form.

## Content Principles

Lead with visual proof. HIKAYA is sold through atmosphere, not long claims.

Explain the process only when it increases trust. The three-step process should be simple, tactile, and visual.

Use AI language sparingly. Preferred wording includes "AI-assisted imagery," "AI-powered photography," and "intelligent visual storytelling." Avoid technical explanations unless asked.

Keep Moroccan references specific. Use zellige, riad courtyards, horseshoe arches, mashrabiya, warm plaster, desert light, blue tile, and handcrafted texture. Do not use vague "oriental" or generic Middle Eastern styling.

Keep the brand bilingual-ready. English is primary for now, Arabic exists in the logo and visual identity, French remains important for Moroccan market expansion, and RTL should remain possible.

## Anti-References

Avoid generic AI agency gloss: no neon gradients, robot icons, synthetic dashboards, AI badges, circuit-board backgrounds, or startup-style blue-purple effects.

Avoid anonymous beige luxury: the site can be warm and minimal, but it must not become silent, cream-on-cream, or detached from HIKAYA's royal-blue identity.

Avoid generic Moroccan decoration: zellige, arches, and patterns should be integrated as structural elements, borders, frames, seals, and texture systems. They should not become tourist-souvenir ornament.

Avoid portfolio templates: no basic square grids with identical cards and no layouts that could belong to any design studio.

Avoid corporate production language: no vendor-speak, no meeting photos, no overlong service bullets, no "we leverage solutions" tone.

Avoid making AI the hero. HIKAYA is a creative agency first; AI is part of the craft, not the personality.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Mobile performance is critical. The primary audience includes Morocco and the MENA region, where visitors may browse on mobile devices with variable network quality. Full-bleed photography should use optimized, lazy-loaded, next-generation formats.

English and French should be supported behind a clean language toggle. Neither language should feel secondary. Arabic is not required as full site copy yet, but the system must remain RTL-ready.

Reduced motion must be supported. Scroll animation, parallax, collage motion, and hover effects should respect `prefers-reduced-motion` and degrade to a fully readable static layout.

Contrast should target WCAG AA minimum, especially royal blue on warm off-white, small navigation labels, image overlays, and text over photography.

The interface should remain navigable without animation, hover, or large image loading.
